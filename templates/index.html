How the consecutive overlays are made

Video requirements

  • A front-facing, mostly stationary camera.
  • A 1280×720-like framing with the pitcher in approximately the same region.
  • A clearly visible pitcher wearing relatively bright clothing against a darker background.
  • The ball becoming visible shortly after release.
  • Similar delivery geometry across pitches.
  • One pitcher and one continuous camera setup per video.

What the tool does

The video is split into individual deliveries using the existing pose-based method. It then creates one synchronized overlay for each adjacent pair in chronological order.

How synchronization works

Each motion-detected delivery is searched only within its likely pre-peak release window. The pitcher’s pose is compared with one common session reference, correcting whole-frame timing differences such as the earlier five-frame mismatch. The background is registered to reduce small camera shifts, and the two synchronized source clips are blended. A freeze is baked into the video at the approximately 150-millisecond hitter decision point; it lasts one second at real-time playback and four seconds in slow-motion mode.

What the tool does not do

It does not calculate ballpaths, tunneling scores, rankings, pitch classifications, or pitch numbers. It adds no labels, tracers, or baseball coloring. The videos are shown in chronological consecutive-pair order.
